Built for Mukilteo

Mukilteo homes, Mukilteo expertise

  • 🔧

    Salt air meets the bluff: corroded caps and rusted dampers

    Mukilteo's waterfront and bluff-top homes sit right in the path of salt-laden marine air off Possession Sound. That salt accelerates rust on steel chimney caps, dampers and liners far faster than inland Snohomish County. We install stainless caps and replace corroded hardware so your chimney stops drawing moisture and stops rotting from the inside out.

  • 🔧

    Old Town's older fireboxes and original masonry

    The historic Old Town and Lighthouse Park area is full of older homes with original brick fireplaces and clay-tile flues. Decades of PNW rain and freeze-thaw cycles crack crowns and loosen mortar joints in these vintage stacks. We tuckpoint, rebuild crowns and reline aging flues to keep these classic Mukilteo chimneys both charming and code-safe.

  • 🔧

    Marine fog and rain: water is the chimney's enemy here

    Mukilteo's near-constant marine layer and heavy rainfall keep masonry damp for months. Water soaks into porous brick and crown concrete, then freezes and pops the surface apart each winter. We waterproof crowns and masonry with vapor-permeable sealant and flash the chimney-to-roof junction so the water stays outside where it belongs.

  • 🔧

    Wood heat on the bluff and chimney-fire risk

    Plenty of Harbour Pointe and bluff-side households lean on wood stoves and fireplaces through the long, damp winters. Burning damp PNW firewood loads flues with creosote fast, and a glazed flue is one spark from a chimney fire. We perform annual sweeps and creosote removal so Mukilteo families can enjoy the fire without the danger.

Your questions, answered

How much does a chimney sweep & inspection cost in Mukilteo?

A standard chimney sweep and inspection in Mukilteo runs about $200 – $400 depending on flue condition, roof access and how much creosote has built up. We give you the price up front before any work begins.

When should I book my chimney service?

Late summer through early fall is ideal — you beat the pre-winter rush and make sure everything's safe before the first fires. We work by appointment, so booking early gets you the best slot.

Are you a 24/7 emergency service?

No. We're a scheduled, by-appointment chimney service. This lets us do thorough, careful work rather than rushed fixes. Book ahead, especially in fall.

Do you protect my home from soot during the work?

Always. We lay drop cloths, seal the firebox and run HEPA vacuums so there's no soot or mess left in your Mukilteo home.

Why is chimney waterproofing so important in Mukilteo?

Our heavy rain and marine moisture soak into masonry, then freeze-thaw cycles crack it apart. Waterproofing the crown and brick is one of the best investments you can make to extend your chimney's life here.
